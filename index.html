About Gold

The Grooming Organization for Liberal Development (GOLD), is a non-profit community-based organization formed in response to the growing challenges facing refugees and marginalized people affected by conflict and isolation.

The Grooming Organization for Liberal Development is dedicated to tackling the pressing challenges faced by refugees and marginalized communities, we want to create a positive impact by restoring hope, empowering marginalized people, and transforming communities into self-reliant , building a sustainable future. Through innovative programs and sustainable solutions, we work to build a future where every individual—regardless of background—has the opportunity to live with dignity, purpose, and resilience.

Our organization is officially registered and recognized by the relevant legal authorities, granting us full legal status to operate within Kakuma Refugee Camp, Kalobeyei Settlement, and the surrounding host communities. This recognition enables us to implement impactful programs across these regions with transparency and accountability. Guided by our founding principles, our obective is clear: to create meaningful opportunities, empower individuals, and transform communities into self-reliant and resilient societies. We are committed to delivering sustainable solutions that uplift the most vulnerable and foster long-term development.

Projects

ClIMATE CHANGE

Community Climate Action & Tree Planting Initiative

Goal:Restore degraded land and improve microclimates through reforestation and environmental education.

Activities:Tree nursery establishment and distribution of drought-resistant seedlings Youth-led climate clubs in schools Community clean-up days and waste recycling campaigns Solar energy education and training Outcomes:Increased green cover Improved community awareness of environmental protection Job creation through nursery and green entrepreneurship .

LIVELIHOODS

Refugee & Host Community Skills for Income (RHCSI)

Goal: Improve self-reliance through vocational training and market linkages.
Activities: Technical training (tailoring, welding, carpentry, solar panel repair) Small business startup kits for graduates Financial literacy and savings group support Market days and business mentorship events Outcomes :New micro-enterprises established Increased household income Strengthened economic inclusion of youth and women.

EDUCATION

Digital and Inclusive Learning Program (DILP)

Goal: Expand access to quality education with a focus on technology and inclusion.
Activities:Equipping learning hubs with tablets, internet, and solar power Digital skills training (basic computer, coding for kids) Remedial learning for out-of-school children and youth Teacher mentorship and inclusive education workshops
Outcomes:Improved digital literacy Increased school retention and performance Reduced gender and disability barriers in education.

Health Programs

Healthy Families, Healthy Futures (HFHF)

Goal: Improve health outcomes for vulnerable populations, especially mothers and children.
Activities:Mobile health education on nutrition, hygiene, and family planning Training community health volunteers (CHVs) Distribution of mosquito nets, reusable sanitary pads, and water filters Monthly child health screening and deworming events
Outcomes: Reduced cases of malnutrition and preventable diseases Better hygiene practices Increased access to reproductive health services.
